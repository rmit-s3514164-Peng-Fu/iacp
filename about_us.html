IACP CODE OF ETHICS AND PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS.

1. INTRODUCTION.

a) This code of Ethics and Professional Standards has been developed for IACP by its director and relates to an expected standard of practice for all Practitioners who are members or become members of IACP.

b) The scope of the standards is to assist in:

· The delivery of quality health care services to the public.

· Defend public health and safety.

· Protect the public interest.

· Promote informed health care choices.

c) The director of IACP reserves the right to alter, amend the Code as and when required, and the members will be notified accordingly.

2. PROPRIETY.

a) Client and public welfare shall be paramount over self- interest of members.

b) Members of IACP will not behave in any manner that will bring IACP or its members into disrepute.2.3

c) Any member that is found to have breached the Code shall have their membership cancelled.

d) Patient confidentiality is paramount, unless an authority to divulge information has been obtained by the member.

3. PROFESSIONAL COMPETENCE.

a) All members shall conduct their practice in a competent and professional manner and shall only practise in the area that they are qualified to practise.

b) No member shall misrepresent his/her level of training or expertise.

4. ACCOUNTABILITY.

a) Members must accept responsibility for their actions and give due consideration to foreseeable consequences of their actions.

b) Members are obligated to advise clients if in the practitioners view the patient requires medical assistance outside of the Members qualifications and expertise.

5. CONSULTATIONS.

a) Client records and information must be maintained and secured in a professional manner.

b) All consulting services provided must be recorded and kept confidential unless the client decides otherwise.

c) Minors must only be treated in the company of an adult.

d) Where there is any concern as to the giving of consent, then consent should be obtained from the client`s legal guardian.

6. CLIENT RELATIONSHIP.

a) No member shall engage in sexual activity with a client being treated by that member.

b) All members will show respect to their client.

c) Members should clearly define and explain their service charges and payment arrangements.

7. PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT, DEVELOPMENT AND INSURANCE.

a) Where a dispute arises amongst members of IACP, the members should attempt to resolve the dispute informally amongst themselves. If this course is unsuccessful, then the matter should be referred to the director of IACP for mediation and resolution.

b) No member shall publicly abuse or denigrate another member.

c) All members shall maintain the required policies of Insurance, both professional and public.

d) Members are required to maintain satisfactory levels of knowledge, learning and professional training, whilst a member of IACP.

8. ADVERTISING.

a) All members are entitled to advertise their membership of IACP, provided they are a current member.

b) Members shall not bring IACP or their profession into disrepute.

c) Members advertising membership of IACP will ensure that such advertising is not misleading.

AIMS OF THE IACP

  1. All IACP members are required to adhere to, and follow and respect with the Constitution of the IACP, and the IACP standards and Code of Ethics completely.
  2. To establish guidelines to make certain that Complementary Practitioners function at the highest level of professional standards.
  3. To create awareness, through knowledge and advertising, to the public about the role of Complementary Practitioners and related healing arts, natural therapies, and health sciences.
  4. Listen to and identify the concerns of the patient, families and carers.
  5. Respect the patient`s rights, and allow them to make decisions about their health care.
  6. Always maintain patient confidentiality as required by Law.
  7. To create a united and national voice for Complementary Practitioners.
  8. To ensure by sharing the knowledge, skill, and goodwill among IACP members raise the overall progress, and also generate community and fellowship among the diverse members of the complementary practices and health field.
  9. To assist and guide each Complementary Practitioner to maintain his/her professional reputation, based on integrity and ability.
  10. Present information accurately and clearly to a patient in accordance with good professional practice.
  11. Maintain and keep accurate up to date patient records in a secure and confidential manner.
  12. Comply with all State and Federal Laws.

IACP - Protecting Members/ Clients Privacy

The International Association of Complementary Practitioners (IACP) will ensure that it providesthe IACP members with the highest quality of service that is including the protection of the privacy of its members.

Collecting the Personal Information.

The IACP may collect your personal information in the following ways:

· Directly from you and your application form

· Member profile form

· Collection of information from publicly available sources

Your Personal Information

Your Personal Information held by the IACP will include, your name, date of birth, current and past addresses, telephone, mobile phone numbers, e-mail address, qualifications/ certificates, insurance policy information, and credit card details. If IACP required such personal information and if you choose not to provide that information, the IACP may not be able to provide you with all of the services you may require.

The IACP will collect some other information includes, but is not limited to, date of joining IACP, your IACP membership number, accredited modalities, certificates issued (The information will hold as a direct consequence of membership).

How IACP Use Your Personal Information

The IACP may be used your personal information:

· For membership application purposes as well as providing the services you require;

· For maintaining and development of systems and infrastructures of the IACP business, that is including testing and upgrading of those infrastructure;

· For administering and managing the services, that is including charging, billing and debt collection;

· For researching and developing the association services;

· For informing you of potential ways the services provided by you could be improved;

· For obtaining, an understanding of the services and needs in order to provide you with a better service.

Also the personal information is collected by the IACP in order to promote and market other services that we consider may be of your interest regarding to the industry, however, the IACP will not utilise your personal information is collected for promoting network marketing businesses, or businesses are considered as “Spam or of a similar nature”. The IACP shall never disclose your personal address information or email address to those parties not approved by you, and you will always be able to unsubscribe from emails that we may send you.

In order to receive your membership certificates/ or renewal certificates via email, the IACP will require you to provide a valid email address so, we can deliver your membership certificate and also we will inform you of the updates regarding to your professional affiliation with the IACP association.

You will always be able to unsubscribe from other services providing you. If you do not wish us to contact you relating other services, please either call us on 1300 000 000 or email us to be removed from our bulk email list at support@theiacp.com.au

By helping the IACP, Ensure Your Personal Information is Correct

The IACP shall take all reasonable preventive measures to ensure that your personal information that collects, utilises and discloses is correct and up to date. The accuracy of the information depends on more information you provide.

If there are any changes or errors in your personal information, advise us in writing or by sending us an email. Ensure to update any changes in your personal information with the IACP that is including your name, residential/ or business address, email address, telephone number, mobile number, and etc.

NAME.

THE International Association of Complementary Practitioners (IACP)

OBJECTS

1. Ensure all the IACP Members adhere to, and follow and respect with the Constitution of the IACP, and the IACP standards and Code of Ethics.

2. Create awareness to the Public of the role of Complementary Practitioners and related healing arts, natural therapies, and health sciences.

3. Promote and encourage the general public to have an understanding of the role of the association with conventional allopathic medical and allied professions.

4. Foster and promote the sharing of knowledge, skill, and goodwill among the IACP members and generate community and fellowship among the diverse members of the complementary practices and health field.

5. Maintain the professional standards of the Association via membership certificate to a recognised International Institute of Complementary Practitioners.

6. Ensure provides affiliation and greater credibility for Complementary Practitioners through their IACP membership.

MEMBERSHIP

The International Association of Complementary Practitioners (IACP) offers Membership for the practices and modalities as detailed on our website: Click Here, and attached to this Constitution.

QUALIFICATION FOR MEMBERSHIP

All applicants must provide Qualification/Certification in at least one of the approved modalities in the IACP website, along with the submitting the IACP`s Membership Application Form and payment of the applicable Membership fee.

APPLICATION FOR MEMBERSHIP

All applicants must submit the IACP Membership application form, along with the required certificate/s in order to become a member of IACP.

Applicants can submit their Application as follows:

1. Via the online application process on IACP website by attaching the supporting documents of certification.

2. Via post, including copies of certificates and the membership fee can be paid directly to the Commonwealth bank. Click here to get the IACP bank details.

MEMBERSHIP FEES

1. Membership fees are to be paid upon submission of the membership application form.

2. Where Membership has lapsed, a practitioner may apply to renew membership, by payment of the appropriate annual membership fee, and obtaining approval of the Director of IACP.

3. Any member can, by notice in writing to the IACP, resign their membership.

4. Membership fees are set each year by the Director of IACP.

RESIGNATION OF A MEMBER

All members desirous of resigning his/her membership can do so at any-time, by informing the Director of IACP, in writing, via email or post, of his/her intention to resign.

REFUND OF MEMBERSHIP FEES

In the event that a member gives notice of his/her intention to resign or terminate membership of the Association within 6 months of joining, the member will be entitled to a full refund of membership fees paid, within 14 days of the notice to resign or terminate.

TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP

The Director of IACP reserves the right to cancel the membership of any member where the director considers that the member is/has been seen to bring the IACP Association, its director and management, its fellow members into disrepute, or has failed to comply with the Association`s Constitution or Code of Ethics.

RESPONSIBILITIES

IACP is not responsible for any fraud, injury, accidents, bankruptcy of any member, or any damage that may occur in the IACP member/s workplace.

ALTERATION OF CONSTITUTION

The IACP Director reserves the right to alter and amend the IACP Constitution as and when, it is deemed required and necessary. All members must be advised of any changes to the Constitution, within 21 days of such change.

IACP Mission Statement

The International Association of Complementary Practitioners mission is to provide membership for all certified Complementary Practitioners, in order to generate a recognised and high standard of practice amongst all health services and complementary healing arts practitioners.

CODE OF ETHICS

All members agree as part of their membership obligations, they are bound by the Associations` Code of Ethics.

ADVERTISING.

Members may advertise in Local newspaper and media outlets regarding their business qualifications and membership of the IACP, but not must mislead or bring the Association into disrepute.
